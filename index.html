These are the webpages for the Evolutionary Quantitative Genetics Workshop, which was to be held June 4 - 9, 2023. In 2023 it would have been an in-person workshop held at the Friday Harbor Laboratories of the University of Washington, in Friday Harbor on San Juan Island. The Workshop has been held yearly since 2011, with the exception of 2020. Since 2017 the in-person workshops have been held at Friday Harbor Laboratories. In 2021 and in 2022, owing to the pandemic, it was held as an online workshop.

The email address for the worshop is fhleqg@gmail.com.

The description of the workshop at the Friday Harbor Laboratories website will be found here

The site you are on right now has a schedule of lectures, computer labs, and discussions. It also has pages for each of these sessions, containing lecture projections, notes, and computer exercises. Currently these are mostly the materials for 2022. For materials from the previous Workshop, go to the "Previous years" link in the heading of this page and follow the links to individual lectures and exercises, and the links to videos of the lectures for the years in which those were posted. The 2023 workshop is co-sponsored by The American Society of Naturalists, the Society of Systematic Biologists, and by the Society for the Study of Evolution.

   

Lecturers

The workshop would have been led by Stevan J. Arnold of Oregon State University and Joe Felsenstein of the University of Washington.

The lecturers would have been:

  • Patrick Carter, Evolutionary Physiology, Washington State University
  • Joe Felsenstein, Genome Sciences / Biology, University of Washington, Seattle
  • Adam Jones, Biological Sciences, University of Idaho
  • Fabio Machado, Integrative Biology, Oklahoma State University
  • Samantha Price, Biological Sciences, Clemson University
  • Jacqueline Sztepanacz, Ecology and Evolutionary Biology, University of Toronto
Stevan Arnold will contribute online lectures.
